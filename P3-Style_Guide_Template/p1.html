Kalmont Mountain Safety & Rescue Team

Project Info

Website - Information Design (Summer 2020)

Softwares/Skills used:

HTML5 + CSS3 + Adobe Photoshop + Figma

Project Description

This is a project required to design an information website for a fictional mountain safety and rescue team. As a non-profit association, this website mainly accounts for popularizing the safety guidelines and rescue knowledge about two topics: Avalanche and Hypothermia, as well as introducing the team itself. For this type of web design, the focus will be on getting the right key guidance information to the user accurately and clearly, while maintaining the aesthetics is second to none. We (the team project) created the mockup in Figma for layout testing and then gradually created the framework. Since it was a multi-person task, we paid a lot of attention to consistency such as the layout, the color selected, and the image style. Then, key information was populated. Finally, by adding interaction patterns such as mouse clicks and dragging, a prototype of a website that can be used for advertising purposes takes shape.

Reflection

From my point of view, we were happy with what we had created, even though we didn't have very good coding skills at the time, so we had to use Figma. This website is the first website design I have ever been involved in, and the early stages of my involvement in a teamwork project. I hope to bring an effective, high-level contribution to the team. Our team is also committed to making a professional and useful website. The layout of each page has the same design language and color scheme, all for the same purpose which is our theme. We conducted formative usability testing at various stages of the design process and received good results and feedback. Our information section uses text and visual elements to collaborate with each other, making it easier for users to read and thus providing a better reading experience. Overall, our website design successfully communicates to users what they want. In the process, I gained a lot of knowledge about information design and the basic rules of web development.
