Soccer Customization for You

Project Info

Website - Brand/Company Design (Fall 2022)

Softwares/Skills used:

HTML5 + CSS3 + JavaScript + Adobe Photoshop

Project Description

This is a project required to design a website for a fictional company we established, “Soccer Customization for You”. This company provides services such as selling the latest soccer cleats and unique cleats customization. As a soccer shoe company that wants to compete among other sports brand majors and resellers, the company not only needs to offer a unique service, the company website design needs to balance the aesthetics of the layout and complete functionality so as to give the user the best experience. According to the feedback, the website refers to the design of big sports brands, which is a simple black-and-white color design style. And the homepage was chosen to include images of custom soccer shoes, which were presented to users using a scrolling format. In order to ensure convenience, the site was kept to a minimum, from the homepage, getting to know us, browsing the products and learning more about them, to the final step of creating an order. Starting with the framework, I have addressed the necessary elements of the page layout. Then advanced features are added, such as images that can be scrolled and product detail images that can be enlarged when clicked. And the site is filled with real content, enough for business use.

Reflection

From my point of view, I am satisfied with my design. The testing of usability was passable because all the features needed for a soccer shoe company's website were implemented, such as a complete product list, an informative product detail page, a minimalistic shopping cart and a payment interface. In terms of design aesthetics, consistency was well implemented, with a minimalist black-and-white style, interspersed with appropriate and distinctive images, and a logical reading layout that worked well with each other to compose the site. The area that deserves improvement would be the mobile version, the part that is the responsibility of the partner to whom I did not contribute much to this project. The site does not adapt well to other devices of different sizes. Overall, I think this project has been a good exercise in user-centered design.
