Soccer Customization for You

Project Info

Website - Brand/Company Design (Fall 2022)

Softwares/Skills used:

HTML5 + CSS3 + JavaScript + Adobe Photoshop

Project Description

This was a project that required the design of a website for a fictional company we built called Soccer Customization for You. This company offers services that include selling the latest soccer shoes and unique soccer shoe customization. As a soccer shoe company that wanted to compete among other big sports brand names and distributors, the company not only needed to offer unique services, the company website design needed to balance the aesthetics of the layout with complete functionality in order to give the user the best experience possible. First, I created a style guide that identified the site's color and font choices, button styles, hover for text and image links, module components, and more. Then created a draft site homepage and product listing page from this. Based on the feedback, the site would reference the design of big sports brands in a simple black and white color design style. Then, after adjusting the style, I created the product detail page, shopping cart and checkout interface. Changes were then made based on the feedback to create a complete shopping process from scratch. Next, key information was filled in and advanced features were added, such as scrollable images and product detail images that could be enlarged when clicked. Finally, after validator checking and testing, a website full of real content that is good enough for commercial use was created.

Reflection

From my point of view, I am satisfied with my design. The testing of usability was passable because all the features needed for a soccer shoe company's website were implemented, such as a complete product list, an informative product detail page, a minimalistic shopping cart and a payment interface. In terms of design aesthetics, consistency was well implemented, with a minimalist black-and-white style, interspersed with appropriate and distinctive images, and a logical reading layout that worked well with each other to compose the site. The area that deserves improvement would be the mobile version, the part that is the responsibility of the partner to whom I did not contribute much to this project. The site does not adapt well to other devices of different sizes. Overall, I think this project has been a good exercise in user-centered design.
